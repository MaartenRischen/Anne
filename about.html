About This Blog

A trusted resource for writers navigating the shifting publishing landscape since 2009.

Our Story

Anne R. Allen's Blog launched in 2009 when Anne repurposed her unpublished columns from Inkwell Newswatch, the online news magazine for writers. What began as a modest effort to share hard-won publishing wisdom quickly found a devoted audience.

Growth accelerated in 2010 after a guest post on Nathan Bransford's popular literary blog introduced Anne's voice to thousands of new readers. Ruth Harris joined as a permanent contributor in 2011, bringing decades of experience as a New York Times bestselling author and former Big 5 editor. Together, they built one of the most respected independent writing blogs on the web.

The blog has been named to Writer's Digest's "Best 101 Websites for Writers" list, a distinction that reflects its commitment to practical, honest guidance for authors at every stage of their journey.

New posts publish weekly on Sundays at approximately 10 AM Pacific Time, covering everything from craft and publishing strategy to scam alerts and the writing life. Whether you're drafting your first novel or navigating your tenth book launch, this blog is for you.

Anne R. Allen

Anne R. Allen

Anne writes comic mystery and romance novels in a style she calls "Chick Lit Noir" — witty, dark-edged stories inspired by the sharp sensibility of Dorothy Parker and the elegant plotting of Dorothy L. Sayers.

Before becoming a full-time author, Anne wore many hats: actress, bookseller, and artistic director of the Patio Playhouse in Escondido, California. She grew up in New England — in Waterville, Maine and the college towns of Connecticut — and graduated from Bryn Mawr College. She now lives on California's beautiful Central Coast, near San Luis Obispo.

Anne has published ten comic mystery novels, a collection of short stories and poems, and co-authored the guidebook The Author Blog with Catherine Ryan Hyde. She is also a contributor to Writer's Digest and the Novel and Short Story Writer's Market.

Ruth Harris

Ruth Harris

Ruth Harris is a multi-million-copy New York Times bestselling author and a Romantic Times award winner for "Best Contemporary." Her books have been translated into 19 languages, published in 25 countries, and selected by the Literary Guild and Book-Of-The-Month Club.

Before her celebrated writing career, Ruth served as a former Big 5 editor and publisher, giving her rare insight into every side of the publishing industry. She grew up on Long Island's North Shore, lived in Manhattan for decades, and has been married over fifty years.

In Memoriam: Shirley S. Allen (1921–2013)

Shirley S. Allen

Shirley S. Allen was Anne's mother — a novelist and scholar whose intellectual curiosity and love of literature left a lasting mark on everyone who knew her. She is remembered here with gratitude and affection.

Her books include the novels Roxanna Britton and Academic Body, as well as the scholarly work Samuel Phelps and Sadler's Wells Theatre.
